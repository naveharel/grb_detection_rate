Settings
Optical survey mode

Requires ≥ i detections in one night (intra-night) or a single detection per night across nights (inter-night). Activates tnight and defines fnight = tnight/24 h; sub-night (intra-night) cadences are rescaled by fnight, per-day (inter-night) cadences are unchanged. Requires fnight ≥ flive.

tOH approximation

Drops tOH from the exposure formula to enable closed-form optimal strategies, while still enforcing texp > tOH as the validity boundary.

Color by detection regime

Tints the surface by dominant regime: warm = flux-limited (DEuc), cool = cadence-limited (Di), gray = singly-limited (Ddec). Brighter = higher range.

Full integral mode

Integrates Rdet over all viewing angles q ∈ [0, qnr] (thesis Eq. 39) instead of using the dominant-term approximation. Slower but more accurate. With sfade or srise > 0 the cuts are applied as a joint uniform-start survival weight here (vs. the best-case hard boundaries of the closed-form mode), so rates sit somewhat lower.

Continuous fading-rate definition

Switches sfade from the discrete finite-difference Δm/Δt across the i detections (default — what real survey alert filters compute) to the instantaneous |dm/dt| at the first detection (strategy-independent). The two coincide in the tcad → 0 limit.
