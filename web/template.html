Settings
Optical survey mode

Requires ≥ i detections in one night or a single detection each night. The tnight slider becomes active and defines fnight = tnight / 24 h. In the continuous (sub-night) cadence regime, the exposure formula uses flive/fnight as the effective live fraction and the total rate is multiplied by fnight; the discrete (per-day) cadence regime is unchanged. Requires fnight ≥ flive, enforced by a dynamic lower bound on the tnight slider.

tOH approximation

Uses the naive texp = flive,eff·tcad/Nexp equation (ignoring tOH in the exposure formula) for fully analytic optimal strategies, while still enforcing flive,eff·tcad/Nexp > tOH as the validity boundary. flive,eff = flive in the discrete (per-day) regime and flive/fnight in the continuous (sub-night) optical-survey regime.

Color by detection regime

Colors the surface by analytical detection regime. Warm colors (red→amber) indicate flux-limited (DEuc) regimes; cool colors (blue→teal) indicate cadence-limited (Di) regimes; gray indicates the singly-limited (Ddec) regime. More vibrant colors correspond to higher detection ranges.

Full integral mode

Computes Rdet using the exact integral over all viewing angles q ∈ [0, qnr] (thesis Eq. 39), instead of the dominant-term approximation. Adds the tail contribution beyond qEuc (flux-limited) or qi (cadence-limited). Slower but more accurate, especially near range boundaries.
