Settings
Optical survey mode

Requires ≥ i detections in one night (intra-night) or a single detection per night across nights (inter-night). Activates tnight and defines fnight = tnight/24 h; sub-night (intra-night) cadences are rescaled by fnight, per-day (inter-night) cadences are unchanged. Requires fnight ≥ flive.

Color by detection regime

Tints the surface by dominant regime: warm = flux-limited (DEuc), cool = cadence-limited (Di), gray = singly-limited (Ddec). Brighter = higher range.

Exact rate mode

Integrates Rdet over all viewing angles q ∈ [0, qnr] (thesis Eq. 39) instead of the dominant-term approximation — slower, but exact. Unlocks the refinements below; the first time it is switched on they all turn on together, and each can then be toggled independently.

Include t− ≈ ton

Measures the detectability window from the light-curve turn-on ton ≈ tp (window start t− ≈ ton) instead of from the burst: t+ ≥ tp + i·tcad, so off-axis events must stay above the limit for the full duration after they peak (the legacy criterion credits detections to the pre-peak interval, where the off-axis flux is negligible). Suppresses the marginal off-axis population and moves the detected q-median on-axis. Combined with “Window spans (i−1)·tcad” gives the corrected criterion t+ − tp ≥ (i−1)·tcad.

Rise cut (srise): random start time

Applies the srise cut as a uniform random observation-start survival weight (only a fraction of bursts pass, depending on when the survey happens to catch them). Off: the best-case hard boundary (first detection assumed at the peak, as in the dominant-term mode), which raises the rate. No effect when srise = 0.

Fade cut (sfade): random start time

Applies the sfade cut as a uniform random observation-start survival weight. Off: the best-case hard boundary, which raises the rate. No effect when sfade = 0.

Other options
tOH approximation

Drops tOH from the exposure formula to enable closed-form optimal strategies, while still enforcing texp > tOH as the validity boundary.

Window spans (i−1)·tcad

Counts the required detectability duration as (i−1)·tcad — the gaps between i detections — instead of i·tcad, wherever it enters (qi, Di). Equivalent to the legacy criterion evaluated at i−1.
